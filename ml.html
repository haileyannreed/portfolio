Credit Card Fraud Detection

This project implements the Balanced Random Forest (BRF) algorithm to detect fraudulent transactions in the highly imbalanced Credit Card Fraud Detection dataset. By applying balanced bootstrapping and unpruned decision trees, the model improves sensitivity to minority class instances (fraud) while maintaining strong overall performance. The approach closely follows the methodology from the paper "Using Random Forest to Learn Imbalanced Data" by Chen, Liaw, and Breiman (2004), and includes evaluation with metrics like F1-score, G-Mean, and Weighted Accuracy.
